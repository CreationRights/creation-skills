# **Section 1: Why Responsible Agent Use Matters**

## **Eyebrow**

WHY IT MATTERS

## **Headline**

Uncontrolled agent use becomes a company problem.

## **Body**

Most companies do not begin with a formal agent strategy.

They begin with useful experiments.

A producer tests an agent for summaries.  
A social team uses one for captions.  
A product team uses one for copy.  
A localization team uses one for translations.  
A legal team sees agent-prepared material late.  
A platform team is asked to connect tools after people are already using them.

That is normal.

The risk appears when those experiments become part of production without ownership, limits, review, or records.

Creation Skills helps companies move from scattered agent use to controlled production support.

# **Section 2: The Creation Skills Position**

## **Eyebrow**

OUR POSITION

## **Headline**

Agents can prepare. People approve.

## **Body**

Creation Skills does not treat agents as creative leaders, legal reviewers, rights owners, or final approvers.

Agents are production support.

They can help teams move faster, organize work, prepare materials, and surface issues. But final judgment remains with people.

## **Principles**

### **Agents should belong to the company.**

The company should own the rules, examples, operating files, review paths, and limits.

### **Every agent needs an owner.**

A person or team must be responsible for reviewing outputs, approving changes, and keeping the agent inside its role.

### **Every agent needs limits.**

Approved tasks and blocked actions should be defined before the agent supports real work.

### **Every agent needs a human gate.**

Sensitive, public, paid, rights-sensitive, talent-facing, client-facing, reused, or final media should not move without human approval.

### **Every important decision needs a record.**

The company should know what was prepared, who reviewed it, why it moved, and what status applied.

# **Section 4: What Agents Should Not Decide**

## **Eyebrow**

BLOCKED ACTIONS

## **Headline**

Good agents need clear no-go areas.

## **Body**

Responsible agent use requires blocked actions.

A production agent should know what it must never do, even if it can generate the output.

## **Blocked Actions**

Cannot publish final media.  
Cannot approve talent usage.  
Cannot approve NILP® usage.  
Cannot change rights terms.  
Cannot approve product claims.  
Cannot bypass legal escalation.  
Cannot override brand review.  
Cannot reuse restricted assets.  
Cannot approve paid media use.  
Cannot approve client-facing decisions.  
Cannot approve final delivery.  
Cannot change operating files without owner approval.  
Cannot create new usage rights.  
Cannot make legal determinations.  
Cannot make final public-facing decisions.

## **Outcome**

Agents remain useful production support without becoming uncontrolled decision makers.

## **Eyebrow**

RIGHTS AND TALENT

## **Headline**

Agents should flag rights-sensitive work, not clear it.

## **Body**

But the agent should not clear those questions.

# **Section 12: Learning With Control**

## **Eyebrow**

LEARNING LOOP

## **Headline**

Agents should improve from approved work, not uncontrolled change.

## **Body**

Production agents can improve over time, but material changes should be controlled.

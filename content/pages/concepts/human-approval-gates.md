# **Section 1: Why Human Approval Matters**

## **Eyebrow**

WHY IT MATTERS

## **Headline**

Faster production still needs clear human control.

## **Body**

Modern media moves through more hands, more files, more tools, more agents, more versions, and more approval points than most companies were built to manage.

When approval is informal, the company depends on memory.

Who checked the asset?  
Who approved the final caption?  
Who reviewed the talent usage?  
Who confirmed the product claim?  
Who cleared the reuse?  
Who decided the media could move?

Human approval gates make those decisions explicit.

## **Core Line**

The agent does the prep.  
The human makes the call.  
The system records both.

# **Section 2: What an Approval Gate Does**

## **Eyebrow**

THE ROLE

## **Headline**

An approval gate decides what can move forward.

## **Body**

A human approval gate is a defined decision point inside the media creation process.

It is the place where a person reviews the prepared work, confirms whether it meets the company’s rules, and decides whether it can move, revise, escalate, or stop.

## **Approval Gates Define**

Who reviews  
Who approves  
What triggers review  
What blocks movement  
What record is required  
What exceptions are allowed  
Where escalation goes  
When Creation Rights should carry the record

## **Outcome**

The company knows who made the decision, why the work moved, and what record should exist before delivery.

# **Section 4: Approval Gate Types**

## **Eyebrow**

GATE TYPES

## **Headline**

Different media decisions need different approval gates.

## **Body**

Creation Skills designs approval gates around the type of work, the risk level, and the record required.

## **Gate Types**

### **Creative Approval**

Confirms that the media meets the creative brief, editorial standard, campaign direction, or production requirement.

### **Brand Approval**

Confirms that the media follows brand rules, tone, claims, visual standards, and public-facing expectations.

### **Legal Approval**

Confirms that legal review is complete where required, including claims, risk, contracts, or sensitive use.

### **Rights Approval**

Confirms that rights, license scope, usage terms, source material, and reuse rules are clear.

### **Talent and NILP® Approval**

Confirms that name, image, likeness, persona, voice, consent, and usage requirements are handled before media moves.

### **Product Claim Approval**

Confirms that product, performance, health, financial, technical, or comparative claims are reviewed before publication.

### **Final Delivery Approval**

Confirms that the asset package, metadata, formats, captions, approvals, and delivery records are complete.

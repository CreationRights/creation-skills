# **Section 1: Why System Repair Matters**

## **Eyebrow**

WHY IT MATTERS

## **Headline**

The expensive problems usually start as small breaks.

## **Body**

Media teams are used to solving problems quickly. A producer finds the file. An editor fixes the format. A social lead rewrites the caption. A legal reviewer catches the usage issue late. A brand lead notices the wrong version before it ships.

That speed is useful, but it can hide the system problem.

If the same breaks keep happening, the company does not need another reminder. It needs a clearer production system.

## **Common Pattern**

The work moves fast.  
The handoff is unclear.  
The owner is assumed.  
The file is incomplete.  
The approval comes late.  
The record is missing.  
The team fixes it manually.  
The same problem happens again.

## **Outcome**

System Repair helps the company find the repeatable control points that prevent the same breaks from returning.

# **Section 2: What Breaks**

## **Eyebrow**

COMMON BREAKS

## **Headline**

Most production breaks happen around files, owners, approvals, rights, and records.

## **Body**

Creation Skills reviews the places where media production slows down, becomes risky, or depends too much on individual memory.

## **Break Categories**

### **Missing Inputs**

Briefs, source files, references, usage terms, product details, captions, transcripts, or approval notes are missing before production begins.

### **Broken Handoffs**

The next owner does not know what they received, what changed, what needs approval, or what must happen before delivery.

### **Wrong Formats**

Assets move forward in the wrong size, channel format, caption structure, file type, language, territory, or delivery package.

### **Late Approval**

Creative, brand, legal, rights, talent, NILP®, product, or client approval happens after production has already moved too far.

### **Agent Drift**

A production agent supports useful work but starts preparing outputs outside its role, limits, operating file, or human gate.

### **Reuse Without Context**

Old assets are cut down, localized, repackaged, translated, or reused without a clear view of the original approval, usage scope, or rights record.

### **Missing Records**

The company cannot easily explain who approved the asset, why it moved, what rights were checked, or what version was delivered.

# **Section 3: Control Points**

## **Eyebrow**

CONTROL POINTS

## **Headline**

Every repeat break needs a control point.

## **Body**

A control point is a practical rule, owner, gate, agent instruction, or record requirement that keeps work from moving in the wrong state.

Control points do not need to slow every production path. They need to stop the right problems at the right moment.

## **Control Point Examples**

### **Missing source file**

Route back to producer before production continues.

### **Wrong export format**

Return to production owner with format requirement.

### **Caption file missing**

Notify social, localization, or accessibility owner.

### **Asset package incomplete**

Block final delivery checklist.

### **Brief changed after production started**

Flag affected owners, agents, and approval gates.

### **Agent exceeds task limit**

Pause the agent output and notify the owner.

### **Rights check incomplete**

Block movement into approval, reuse, publication, or delivery.

### **Approval owner unclear**

Route to the named decision owner before work moves.

### **Record missing**

Hold final delivery until required approval, rights, or metadata record is complete.

# **Section 4: Production Agents and System Repair**

## **Eyebrow**

PRODUCTION AGENTS

## **Headline**

Agents can help catch breaks, but only inside limits.

## **Body**

Company-owned production agents can help identify missing inputs, incomplete packages, format issues, naming problems, and review gaps.

But agents should not decide what can move forward on their own.

Creation Skills defines where agents can flag, route, summarize, prepare, or check, and where a person must approve, reject, revise, or escalate.

## **Agents May Help Flag**

Missing source files  
Incomplete asset packages  
Wrong naming conventions  
Missing captions  
Format mismatches  
Brief changes  
Delivery checklist gaps  
Metadata gaps  
Unclear reuse requests  
Review steps not completed  
Rights check missing  
Agent output outside task

## **Agents Must Not**

Approve final media  
Approve talent usage  
Change rights terms  
Override brand review  
Approve product claims  
Bypass legal escalation  
Reuse restricted assets  
Approve NILP® usage  
Make final client-facing decisions  
Change operating files without owner approval

## **Core Line**

The agent does the prep.  
The human makes the call.  
The system records both.

# **Section 5: Operating Files and Repair Rules**

## **Eyebrow**

OPERATING FILES

## **Headline**

The repair rule should live inside the operating file.

## **Body**

If a break keeps happening, the response should not depend on memory.

Creation Skills helps companies update operating files so agents and teams know what to do when common problems appear.

## **Repair Rules May Define**

What the agent should flag  
Who owns the issue  
When work must pause  
Where the issue routes  
What information is missing  
What record is required  
What cannot move forward  
When escalation is required  
When the operating file should be updated

## **Example Rule**

If caption files are missing before final delivery, the delivery package pauses, the social or accessibility owner is notified, and the package cannot move forward until captions are added or an exception is approved.

## **Outcome**

The company turns repeated production problems into visible rules.

WHY IT MATTERS

## **Headline**

A producer thinks brand approved it.

The work moves, but no one can clearly explain who made the decision.

# **Section 3: Approval Ownership**

## **Eyebrow**

OWNERSHIP

## **Headline**

Approval should not belong to “the team.”

## **Body**

A team can contribute to review, but a decision needs an owner.

Creation Skills helps companies define named owners for the decisions that control media movement.

## **Owner Types**

### **Production Owner**

Owns the production path, asset package, handoffs, and delivery readiness.

### **Creative Owner**

Owns creative quality, concept fit, editorial direction, and final creative judgment.

### **Brand Owner**

Owns public-facing brand fit, tone, visual standards, product language, and market consistency.

### **Legal or Rights Owner**

Owns legal escalation, rights review, usage questions, consent, NILP®, and related review points.

### **Talent Owner**

Owns talent-facing review, creator deliverables, athlete or artist approvals, model usage, and identity-sensitive material.

### **Product Owner**

Owns product facts, feature accuracy, claim review, marketplace content, and commerce language.

### **Platform Owner**

Owns metadata, system status, access, platform entry, asset lifecycle, and Creation Rights readiness.

### **Final Approver**

Owns the final decision before media moves into delivery, publication, reuse, paid media, or platform governance.

# **Section 6: Escalation Paths**

## **Eyebrow**

ESCALATION

## **Headline**

Unclear work needs a route, not a guess.

## **Body**

Escalation paths define where work goes when the normal approval path is not enough.

Escalation should not feel like failure. It is how the company protects the work before it moves too far.

## **Escalation May Be Needed When**

The asset contains talent or NILP®.  
Consent is unclear.  
Rights information is missing.  
The agent output exceeds its task.  
A product claim needs review.  
The asset is being reused in a new context.  
A new territory or channel is added.  
Paid media is planned.  
Sponsor use is involved.  
Archive material is used.  
A client or partner wants a new version.  
A final delivery package is incomplete.  
The approval owner is unclear.

## **Escalation Path**

Pause movement.  
Identify the issue.  
Route to the right owner.  
Capture the decision.  
Approve, revise, or block.  
Update the operating file if needed.  
Record the outcome.

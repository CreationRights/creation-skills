# **Section 2: What Rights Review Covers**

## **Eyebrow**

RIGHTS REVIEW

## **Headline**

Rights review is about use, scope, source, and approval.

## **Body**

Creation Skills helps companies define when rights review should happen and what information teams need before media moves forward.

## **Rights Review Areas**

### **Source Material**

Where the media came from, who owns it, what permissions exist, and whether third-party material is included.

### **Usage Scope**

What use is allowed across duration, territory, channel, format, paid media, organic social, partner use, client use, and reuse.

### **Talent and Identity**

Whether a person’s name, image, likeness, persona, voice, performance, or identifiable presence appears in the media.

### **Consent**

Whether the person or rights holder has provided permission for the intended use.

### **Reuse**

Whether an existing asset can be cut down, localized, translated, repackaged, boosted, refreshed, or moved into a new context.

### **Approval Records**

What record should exist before media moves forward.

# **Section 3: What NILP® Means Here**

## **Eyebrow**

NILP®

## **Headline**

NILP® checks protect identity-sensitive media.

## **Body**

NILP® means **Name, Image, Likeness, and Persona**.

A NILP® check matters when media uses a person’s identity, likeness, voice, persona, performance, creator content, behind-the-scenes material, or recognizable presence.

Creation Skills helps companies define where those checks belong inside the company flow.

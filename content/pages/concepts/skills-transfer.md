WHY SKILLS MATTER

## **Headline**

# **Section 3: Role-Specific Skills**

## **Eyebrow**

ROLE-SPECIFIC CAPABILITY

## **Headline**

Different teams need different skills around the same company flow.

## **Body**

Creation Skills helps each team understand its role in the media creation process.

The goal is not for everyone to know everything. The goal is for the right people to know what they own, when they approve, when they escalate, and how the work moves.

## **Role Areas**

### **Creative Teams**

Understand how production agents support creative preparation, where human judgment remains required, and how approved examples improve the system.

### **Production Teams**

Manage files, handoffs, delivery packages, agent outputs, operating files, and production-path discipline.

### **Brand Teams**

Review brand-sensitive outputs, product claims, tone, market-specific use, public-facing media, and final campaign materials.

### **Legal and Rights Teams**

Define rights review triggers, escalation points, reuse controls, NILP® checks, consent requirements, and record expectations.

### **Talent and Creator Teams**

Review name, image, likeness, persona, voice, consent, talent-facing materials, and usage-sensitive media.

### **Operations Teams**

Maintain the company flow, track adoption, support owner roles, monitor broken handoffs, and prepare the organization for Creation Rights.

### **Technology and Platform Teams**

Understand where production agents, operating files, approval gates, protected records, and Creation Rights platform governance connect.

# **Section 9: Skills Transfer Inside Engagements**

## **Eyebrow**

HOW IT HAPPENS

## **Headline**

Skills are transferred through the work.

## **Body**

Creation Skills does not separate skills from implementation.

The transfer happens while the company reviews its current process, designs the new model, builds agent rules, tests production paths, and prepares for Creation Rights.

## **Engagement Paths**

### **C/Skills Review**

Teams learn where the current company flow is strong, where it breaks, and what needs clearer ownership.

### **Operating Model Design**

Teams learn the future model, role ownership, production paths, approval gates, rights review points, and record requirements.

### **Agent Operating Blueprint**

Agent owners learn how to supervise production agents, maintain operating files, review outputs, and control updates.

### **Governance Blueprint**

Decision owners learn what they approve, when review triggers, and what needs to be recorded.

### **Pilot Transformation**

Teams practice the model on one real production path before wider rollout.

### **Implementation Office**

Teams receive ongoing owner guidance, agent supervision, operating-file updates, adoption support, and Creation Rights readiness support.

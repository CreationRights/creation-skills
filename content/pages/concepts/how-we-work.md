# **Section 1: The Method**

## **Eyebrow**

THE METHOD

## **Headline**

Review. Design. Blueprint. Pilot. Implement. Govern.

## **Body**

Creation Skills uses a practical method built around real media production.

The work starts small enough to understand and becomes structured enough to scale.

## **Method Steps**

### **1\. Review**

Understand the current company flow behind one production path.

### **2\. Design**

Define how the production path should move through people, agents, approvals, rights checks, delivery, reuse, and records.

### **3\. Blueprint**

Create the operating files, production-agent rules, approval gates, escalation paths, and record requirements.

### **4\. Pilot**

Test the model on one real or representative production path.

### **5\. Implement**

Support adoption, owner guidance, agent supervision, operating-file updates, and Creation Rights readiness.

### **6\. Govern**

Refine the rules as the company learns from real production.

# **Section 14: What We Do Not Do**

## **Eyebrow**

BOUNDARIES

## **Headline**

Clear boundaries keep the work focused.

## **Body**

Creation Skills has a specific role inside the Creation Rights ecosystem.

## **Boundary Cards**

### **Not Generic Tool Consulting**

We do not simply recommend another tool. We design the company process around people, agents, approvals, rights, records, and platform readiness.

### **Not a Creative Agency**

We do not take over the company’s creative output. We help the company control its own production system.

### **Not a Public Course Provider**

Formal education and certification belong with CreativeIP.org.

### **Not Legal Counsel**

Creation Skills supports process design, review points, approval gates, and record requirements. Legal decisions remain with the client and its advisors.

### **Not the Platform**

Creation Rights remains the platform for rights, NILP®, consent, approvals, metadata, audit, payouts, and compliance-sensitive media records.

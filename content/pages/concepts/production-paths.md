# **Section 1: Why Production Paths Matter**

## **Eyebrow**

WHY IT MATTERS

## **Headline**

The same work repeats. The system around it often does not.

## **Body**

Most media teams are not only making one asset at a time.

They are producing, cutting down, translating, versioning, packaging, approving, delivering, and reusing media across many teams and channels.

The problem is that each production path often depends on informal knowledge.

Who has the source files?  
Who owns the brief?  
Which agent can prepare the first pass?  
Who approves the output?  
Where does rights review sit?  
What record should exist before delivery?  
Can this asset be reused later?

Creation Skills helps companies turn repeated production paths into company-owned systems.

# **Section 2: What a Production Path Includes**

## **Eyebrow**

THE STRUCTURE

## **Headline**

A production path should show how media moves from input to approval.

## **Body**

Creation Skills looks at each production path as a full company system.

## **Production Path Components**

### **Inputs**

Briefs, references, source files, rights notes, product details, transcripts, talent information, campaign instructions, and delivery requirements.

### **People**

Creative owners, production owners, brand reviewers, legal reviewers, rights owners, talent reviewers, agent owners, and delivery owners.

### **Production Agents**

Company-owned agents that prepare drafts, captions, clips, translations, notes, summaries, versions, checklists, or packages.

### **Operating Files**

The company-owned files that define the task, approved examples, blocked actions, output formats, naming rules, review steps, and escalation notes.

### **Human Approval Gates**

The points where people review, approve, revise, escalate, or stop the work.

### **Rights and Reuse Checks**

The review points for rights, NILP®, consent, usage scope, territory, channel, format, talent use, metadata, and reuse.

### **Records**

The approval, rights, metadata, delivery, and audit records that need to exist before media moves forward.

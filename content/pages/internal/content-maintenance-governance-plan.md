# **Creation Skills Internal Page: Content Maintenance and Governance Plan**

## **Page Identity**

**Creation Skills**  
**C/Skills.**  
**A Creation Rights Division**

## **Document Type**

Internal content governance document

## **Public Status**

Not a public page

## **Purpose**

This page defines how Creation Skills website content should be maintained after launch.

The goal is to keep the site accurate, premium, legally cautious, and aligned with the Creation Rights ecosystem as new pages, resources, audience pages, concept pages, and form paths are added.

Creation Skills has a deep content library. That library should be managed carefully so the public site does not drift into generic AI consulting, public course language, legal advice, platform claims, or cluttered navigation.

---

# **1\. Governance Principle**

## **Headline**

The site should stay controlled as it grows.

## **Body**

Creation Skills should grow through deliberate publishing, not page sprawl.

Every new page should support the core offer:

Creation Skills helps companies prepare the company flow behind modern media creation through people, production agents, operating files, human approval gates, rights review, protected records, and Creation Rights readiness.

## **Core Rule**

Do not publish a page just because it exists in the master document.

Publish a page only when it supports the strategy, has a clear CTA, passes review, and fits the current site structure.

---

# **2\. Source of Truth**

## **Eyebrow**

SOURCE OF TRUTH

## **Headline**

The master document controls the content library.

## **Body**

The Creation Skills master document is the source of truth for approved page content, internal planning pages, publishing queues, metadata, legal-review notes, and build prompts.

The website should be built from the approved launch set, not from every page in the master document.

## **Source Hierarchy**

### **1\. Master Document**

Holds the complete content library.

### **2\. Launch Sitemap**

Controls what is built and published first.

### **3\. Menu Structure**

Controls what appears in navigation.

### **4\. Page Status Index**

Controls page status, URL, review needs, and publishing readiness.

### **5\. Build File Structure and Route Map**

Controls implementation.

### **6\. QA Checklist**

Controls final review before launch or update.

## **Rule**

If the master document and the live site disagree, update the master document first, then update the site.

---

# **3\. Page Ownership**

## **Eyebrow**

OWNERSHIP

## **Headline**

Every page should have an owner.

## **Body**

Each page should have a named owner responsible for accuracy, updates, review status, and publishing decisions.

## **Ownership Roles**

### **Content Owner**

Responsible for page copy, positioning, tone, and clarity.

### **Design Owner**

Responsible for page layout, visual consistency, and brand feel.

### **Development Owner**

Responsible for route, component use, metadata, performance, and technical implementation.

### **Legal Reviewer**

Responsible for legal-sensitive content, privacy, terms, cookies, notices, rights language, NILP® language, and no-legal-advice boundaries.

### **SEO Reviewer**

Responsible for metadata, URL, indexing, internal links, and search intent.

### **Final Approver**

Responsible for deciding whether the page can publish.

## **Rule**

No new public page should publish without a content owner and final approver.

---

# **4\. Page Status Management**

## **Eyebrow**

STATUS

## **Headline**

Each page needs a current status.

## **Status Labels**

### **Draft**

Content exists but is not ready for review.

### **Content Review**

Page is being checked for clarity, positioning, and tone.

### **Legal Review**

Page is with counsel or needs legal-sensitive review.

### **Design Ready**

Copy is approved and ready for design.

### **Build Ready**

Copy and design direction are approved for implementation.

### **Staged**

Page is built on staging but not public.

### **Approved for Launch**

Page is ready to publish.

### **Published**

Page is live.

### **Hold**

Page should not publish yet.

### **Retired**

Page has been removed from the public site but preserved in the master document.

## **Rule**

A page should not move from Draft to Published without passing the required review steps.

Major changes require content review and, where relevant, legal review before publication.

# **7\. Ecosystem Boundary Review**

## **Eyebrow**

ECOSYSTEM BOUNDARIES

## **Headline**

Do not blur Creation Skills, Creation Rights, and CreativeIP.org.

## **Correct Split**

### **Creation Skills**

The consulting and transformation layer.

Prepares the company flow, production paths, production agents, operating files, human approval gates, rights review points, protected records, and Creation Rights readiness.

### **Creation Rights**

The platform layer.

Governs rights, NILP®, consent, talent use, metadata, approvals, audit, payouts, and compliance-sensitive media records.

### **CreativeIP.org**

The education and standards layer.

Supports public education, professional development, certification, glossary, reference materials, and shared literacy around creative intellectual property.

## **Required Line**

Creation Skills prepares the company.  
CreativeIP.org educates selected leaders.  
Creation Rights governs the protected layer.

## **Rule**

Every page that mentions the ecosystem should preserve this split.

# **8\. Legal-Sensitive Content Review**

## **Eyebrow**

LEGAL-SENSITIVE CONTENT

## **Headline**

Rights, NILP®, consent, records, privacy, and claims language need extra care.

## **Pages Requiring Care**

Privacy Notice  
Terms of Use  
Cookie Policy  
Legal Notice / Company Information  
Confidentiality and Client Materials  
Rights Review and NILP® Checks  
Protected Records and Metadata  
Asset Reuse and Usage Controls  
Responsible Agent Use  
Legal, Rights, and Risk Teams  
Why Rights Review Belongs Earlier

## **Review Questions**

Does the page imply Creation Skills provides legal advice?

Does the page imply Creation Skills clears rights?

Does the page imply agents make legal determinations?

Does the page imply guaranteed compliance?

Does the page overpromise confidentiality before an agreement?

Does the page collect sensitive information through public forms?

Does the page confuse consulting with platform governance?

Does the page need counsel review before publishing?

## **Required Boundary**

Creation Skills supports process design. It does not replace legal counsel. Legal decisions remain with the client and its advisors.

# **9\. Form Governance**

## **Eyebrow**

FORMS

## **Headline**

Forms should stay safe as the site grows.

## **Body**

Forms are one of the highest-risk parts of the website because users may try to submit sensitive materials.

## **Form Rules**

No public file uploads unless specifically approved.

No confidential material collection through public forms.

No credentials or access tokens.

No legal document uploads.

No private talent material uploads.

No sensitive rights-note uploads.

No unnecessary sensitive personal data.

No information about minors requested.

## **Required Warning**

Do not send confidential source files, unreleased assets, private talent materials, contracts, legal documents, credentials, private links, or sensitive rights notes through this form.

## **Rule**

Any new form field should be reviewed before launch.

# **11\. Publishing Cadence**

## **Eyebrow**

PUBLISHING CADENCE

## **Headline**

Publish in controlled batches.

## **Body**

Publishing too many pages at once can make the site harder to review, harder to QA, and less focused.

## **Recommended Cadence**

One to three pages per release batch.

Each batch should have:

Content review  
SEO review  
Internal links  
CTA check  
Legal review if needed  
Design QA  
Staging QA  
Launch decision

## **Rule**

Do not publish a batch unless every page in the batch has a clear purpose and owner.

# **12\. Quarterly Content Review**

## **Eyebrow**

REVIEW CADENCE

## **Headline**

Review the site regularly after launch.

## **Monthly Review**

Check form submissions.  
Check lead quality.  
Check top pages.  
Check broken links.  
Check 404s.  
Check CTA performance.  
Check page feedback.  
Check if any copy is confusing visitors.

## **Quarterly Review**

Review all public pages.  
Confirm legal pages are current.  
Confirm privacy and cookie tools match policies.  
Review analytics setup.  
Review top navigation.  
Review footer links.  
Review resource performance.  
Update phase two and phase three publishing priorities.  
Retire or revise weak pages.

## **Annual Review**

Review the whole master document.  
Review ecosystem positioning.  
Review legal disclaimers.  
Review trademark notices.  
Review service descriptions.  
Review forms and data collection.  
Review page status index.  
Review build prompts and design direction.

# **13\. Content Retirement**

## **Eyebrow**

RETIREMENT

## **Headline**

Some pages may need to be removed or held.

## **Reasons to Retire or Hold a Page**

The service is no longer offered.

The page creates confusion.

The page overlaps too much with another page.

The legal risk is too high.

The page is outdated.

The page receives the wrong kind of enquiries.

The page makes Creation Skills sound generic.

The page blurs Creation Skills with Creation Rights or CreativeIP.org.

## **Retirement Process**

Mark page as Retired in the master document.

Remove from navigation and sitemap.

Redirect if the page was public and indexed.

Preserve the original copy in the master document.

Update internal links.

Update SEO map.

# **Creation Skills Internal Page: Page Cutdown Plan**

## **Page Identity**

**Creation Skills**  
**C/Skills.**  
**A Creation Rights Division**

## **Document Type**

Internal launch planning document

## **Public Status**

Not a public page

## **Purpose**

This page defines how to cut down the full Creation Skills content library into a focused launch site.

The master document should keep every page.

The public website should launch with only the pages needed to explain the offer, build trust, route serious enquiries, and support a C/Skills Review request.

The goal is to avoid launching a site that feels too large, too heavy, or too scattered.

Creation Skills should feel premium, selective, creative, and serious.

---

# **1\. Cutdown Principle**

## **Headline**

The master doc can be deep. The launch site should be sharp.

## **Body**

The full Creation Skills content library is intentionally large.

It gives the brand strategic depth across consulting, production agents, operating files, approval gates, rights review, NILP®, protected records, audience pages, resources, legal utilities, and ecosystem pages.

But not every page should launch at once.

A large launch site can make the offer feel unclear.

A tight launch site helps visitors understand:

What Creation Skills is.  
What problem it solves.  
How the engagements work.  
Who it helps.  
How it connects to Creation Rights.  
How to request a C/Skills Review.

## **Rule**

Launch what converts.  
Stage what explains.  
Hold what expands.

# **13\. Final Recommendation**

## **Headline**

Launch the focused version first.

## **Body**

The best first version of Creation Skills should feel clear, premium, and confident.

It should not feel like a giant content archive.

The launch site should explain:

Creation Skills is consulting.  
Creation Rights is the platform.  
CreativeIP.org is education and standards.  
Creation Skills starts with one production path.  
The work centers on people, production agents, operating files, human approval gates, rights review, protected records, and Creation Rights readiness.

## **Core Line**

The agent does the prep.  
The human makes the call.  
The system records both.

## **Final Rule**

Build the launch site from the cutdown.  
Keep the full library in the master doc.  
Publish the depth gradually.

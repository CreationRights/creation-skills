# **Creation Skills Internal Page: Analytics and Measurement Plan**

## **Page Identity**

**Creation Skills**  
**C/Skills.**  
**A Creation Rights Division**

## **Document Type**

Internal analytics and measurement planning document

## **Public Status**

Not a public page

## **Purpose**

This page defines how Creation Skills should measure website performance, form intent, content engagement, and conversion quality after launch.

The goal is not to over-track visitors.

The goal is to understand whether the site helps serious companies understand Creation Skills, explore the right engagement, and request a C/Skills Review.

Analytics should be privacy-aware, legally reviewed, and aligned with the final Privacy Notice and Cookie Policy.

---

# **1\. Measurement Principle**

## **Headline**

Measure the path to serious enquiry.

## **Body**

Creation Skills is not a high-volume consumer website.

It is a premium consulting and transformation site for serious companies working around media creation, production agents, operating files, approval gates, rights review, protected records, and Creation Rights readiness.

The most important measurement is not raw traffic.

The most important measurement is whether the right visitors understand the offer and take the right next step.

## **Core Measurement Question**

Are the right visitors moving from understanding to enquiry?

# **12\. Privacy and Cookie Alignment**

## **Eyebrow**

PRIVACY

## **Headline**

Analytics must match the Privacy Notice and Cookie Policy.

## **Body**

Before launch, confirm which tools are actually being used.

The Privacy Notice and Cookie Policy should reflect:

Analytics tools  
Form tools  
CRM tools  
Scheduling tools  
Hosting tools  
Cookie banner or consent tool  
Marketing or retargeting tools if any

## **Privacy Rules**

Do not add advertising or retargeting tools without updating the Cookie Policy.

Do not collect unnecessary personal information.

Do not track sensitive form message content in analytics.

Do not expose private enquiry data in analytics dashboards.

Do not treat form submissions as public data.

## **Counsel Note**

Final analytics and cookie setup should be reviewed against applicable privacy requirements before publication.

# **14\. Review Cadence**

## **Eyebrow**

CADENCE

## **Headline**

Review analytics in small cycles.

## **Launch Review Schedule**

### **Week 1**

Check form submissions, broken links, 404s, and top landing pages.

### **Week 2**

Review CTA clicks, form starts, and form drop-off.

### **Month 1**

Review page performance, lead quality, and messaging clarity.

### **Month 2**

Decide whether to publish resources or phase two concept pages.

### **Month 3**

Review which audience pages should be prioritized.

## **Rule**

Use analytics to improve clarity, not to chase random traffic.

# **15\. Decision Rules**

## **Eyebrow**

DECISIONS

## **Headline**

Use measurement to decide what to improve next.

## **If Visitors View Pages but Do Not Click CTAs**

Improve CTA placement, page clarity, or final CTA language.

## **If Visitors Click Book Review but Do Not Submit**

Improve form length, form clarity, trust language, or confidentiality note.

## **If Many Visitors Ask Generic AI Questions**

Clarify that Creation Skills is not generic AI consulting.

## **If Many Visitors Ask for Legal Advice**

Strengthen legal boundary and rights review process language.

## **If Many Visitors Ask for Software Demo**

Clarify Creation Skills vs Creation Rights platform.

## **If Creation Rights Readiness Performs Well**

Consider promoting it more in the homepage and footer.

## **If Agent Operating Blueprint Performs Well**

Consider publishing Company-Owned Production Agents and Operating Files pages sooner.

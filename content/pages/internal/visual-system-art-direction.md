# **Creation Skills Internal Page: Visual System and Art Direction**

## **Page Identity**

**Creation Skills**  
**C/Skills.**  
**A Creation Rights Division**

## **Document Type**

Internal design direction document

## **Public Status**

Not a public page

## **Purpose**

This page defines the visual system for Creation Skills.

The goal is to make the brand feel creative, premium, editorial, and enterprise-ready without drifting into generic AI consulting, law firm design, software dashboard design, or public education branding.

Creation Skills should feel like a creative industry consulting practice with a strong lime lab identity.

# **3\. Color Direction**

## **Headline**

Lime green is the creative signal.

## **Primary Color**

### **Lime Green**

Use as the main identity color.

It should feel energetic, creative, sharp, and modern.

Use for:

CTA accents  
Graphic marks  
Section labels  
Linework  
Cards  
System diagrams  
Approval gate markers  
Agent nodes  
Hover states  
Editorial highlights

## **Supporting Colors**

### **Deep Charcoal**

Use for main text, deep backgrounds, footer, and high-contrast sections.

### **Warm White**

Use for primary page backgrounds.

The site should not feel cold or sterile.

### **Soft Neutral**

Use for cards, section backgrounds, utility surfaces, and form panels.

### **Muted Gray**

Use for secondary text, borders, metadata labels, and supporting UI details.

### **Lime Tint**

Use for subtle backgrounds, cards, and callout blocks.

## **Color Rules**

Lime should feel intentional, not decorative.

Do not use lime only as a button color.

Use lime as part of the full identity system: lines, labels, symbols, marks, and visual structure.

Keep contrast strong.

Do not place small lime text on white if it hurts readability.

# **4\. Typography Direction**

## **Headline**

Typography should feel editorial and precise.

## **Type Personality**

Confident  
Sharp  
Readable  
Modern  
Editorial  
Premium  
Structured

## **Headline Style**

Large, direct, and spacious.

Headlines should feel like strategic statements, not marketing fluff.

## **Body Style**

Readable, calm, and clear.

The content is complex, so typography must make it easier to understand.

## **Eyebrow Style**

Small uppercase labels.

Use for:

Page category  
Section type  
Engagement type  
Audience type  
Trust labels  
Internal planning labels

## **Typography Rules**

Avoid cramped text.

Avoid tiny gray body copy.

Avoid overly playful fonts.

Avoid generic startup typography.

Avoid making pages feel like a course module.

Use strong contrast between headline, subhead, and body.

## **Headline**

The layout should feel spacious, modular, and editorial.

## **Layout Principles**

Large hero sections  
Generous negative space  
Sharp section breaks  
Clear reading flow  
Alternating content density  
Modular card systems  
Split panels  
Process rows  
Strong final CTAs  
Footer with clear ecosystem structure

## **Section Types**

### **Editorial Hero**

Large headline, strong subhead, focused body copy, two CTAs, and optional abstract system visual.

### **Split Panel**

Text on one side, visual or card stack on the other.

### **Card Grid**

Used for engagements, audiences, resources, topics, and deliverables.

### **Process Path**

Used for review, design, blueprint, pilot, implement, govern.

### **Principle Block**

Used for core lines:

The agent does the prep.  
The human makes the call.  
The system records both.

### **Ecosystem Split**

Used for Creation Skills, Creation Rights, and CreativeIP.org relationship.

### **Final CTA**

Strong closing section with one clear next action.

## **Layout Rules**

Do not make every section a card grid.

Do not make the site feel like a dashboard.

Do not overuse icons.

Do not crowd sections with too many small labels.

Let the page breathe.

# **6\. Graphic Motifs**

## **Headline**

The visual language should come from media movement and control.

## **Motifs to Use**

### **Agent Nodes**

Small connected marks representing production agents.

Should look abstract and controlled, not like robots.

### **Human Approval Gates**

Checkpoint marks, gates, stamps, or decision nodes.

Use to show where people approve before media moves.

### **Operating Files**

File cards, rule sheets, structured notes, prompt blocks, blocked-action lists, owner fields.

### **Rights Review Marks**

Small review labels around NILP®, consent, usage scope, talent, rights, reuse, and paid media.

### **Protected Records**

Metadata cards, record blocks, audit references, approval fields, asset status cards.

### **Asset Flow Lines**

Routing lines showing media moving from brief to delivery.

### **Cut Lines**

Editorial crop lines, production marks, frame edges, versioning marks.

### **Annotation Marks**

Small notes, arrows, labels, or brackets that give a zine/editorial feel.

## **Motifs to Avoid**

Robots  
Brain graphics  
Generic AI sparkles  
Cloud network clichés  
Stock office scenes  
Gavel or law icons  
Graduation caps  
Crypto-style nodes  
Overly literal padlocks  
Busy fake dashboards

No fake unreadable text.

No random AI glow.

No generic 3D objects.

No sterile enterprise dashboard.

No law firm imagery.

No classroom imagery.

# **11\. Form Page Visual Direction**

## **Headline**

Forms should feel safe, serious, and easy to complete.

## **Form Design Rules**

Use clear labels.

Use enough spacing.

Use no file upload at launch.

Show confidentiality warning.

Make the primary CTA clear.

Keep the form visually calm.

Do not make the form look like a lead-gen trap.

## **Form Panel Style**

Warm white or soft neutral panel.  
Sharp border.  
Clear section labels.  
Strong submit button.  
Confidentiality warning in a visible but not alarming style.

## **Required Warning**

Do not send confidential source files, unreleased assets, private talent materials, contracts, legal documents, credentials, private links, or sensitive rights notes through this form.

# **13\. Mobile Direction**

## **Headline**

Mobile should feel just as premium as desktop.

## **Mobile Rules**

Keep navigation simple.

Keep CTA visible.

Stack cards cleanly.

Avoid tiny text.

Avoid too many side-by-side visual details.

Do not use busy backgrounds behind body copy.

Keep forms easy to complete.

Ensure process steps are readable.

Make final CTA strong.

## **Mobile Priority**

A visitor should understand in under one minute:

What Creation Skills does.  
Who it helps.  
Why Creation Rights readiness matters.  
How to book a C/Skills Review.

# **14\. Design Do and Do Not**

## **Do**

Use lime as an identity system.

Use strong typography.

Use negative space.

Use abstract media-system visuals.

Use clear card systems.

Use human approval gate motifs.

Use protected record motifs.

Use editorial structure.

Use premium restraint.

## **Do Not**

Use generic AI gradients.

Use robots.

Use stock corporate photography.

Use law firm imagery.

Use course or academy visuals.

Use unreadable fake text.

Use dashboard-heavy UI.

Use random icons everywhere.

Use cluttered zine style that hurts readability.

Use black and lime in a way that feels like Deloitte.

# **Creation Skills Internal Page: Form Routing and Intake Plan**

## **Page Identity**

**Creation Skills**  
**C/Skills.**  
**A Creation Rights Division**

## **Document Type**

Internal form and intake planning document

## **Public Status**

Not a public page

## **Purpose**

This page defines how Creation Skills website forms should work, what information they should collect, where submissions should route, which confirmation pages should appear, and what safety language must be included.

The goal is to keep public intake clear, useful, and safe.

Creation Skills should not collect confidential production materials, unreleased assets, contracts, private talent materials, credentials, or sensitive rights notes through public website forms.

---

# **1\. Form Principle**

## **Headline**

Describe first. Share later through the right process.

## **Body**

Creation Skills works with sensitive company processes, production paths, talent materials, rights review, NILP® checks, protected records, and Creation Rights readiness.

That does not mean sensitive materials should be submitted through public forms.

Public forms should collect enough context to understand the enquiry and route it correctly.

They should not collect files or confidential materials at launch.

## **Core Rule**

No public file uploads at launch.

## **Required Warning**

Do not send confidential source files, unreleased assets, private talent materials, contracts, legal documents, credentials, private links, or sensitive rights notes through this form.

# **9\. Confidentiality and Safety Rules**

## **Headline**

Forms must protect the client and Creation Skills.

## **Public Form Rules**

No file uploads at launch.  
No confidential-material collection.  
No contract upload.  
No talent material upload.  
No credential collection.  
No private-link request.  
No legal advice intake.  
No sensitive rights review inside a public form.  
No personal information about minors.  
No unnecessary sensitive personal data.

## **Required Form Warning**

Do not send confidential source files, unreleased assets, private talent materials, contracts, legal documents, credentials, private links, or sensitive rights notes through this form.

## **Optional Longer Warning**

Public forms are for enquiries only. If sensitive materials are needed later, Creation Skills can discuss the right client process before anything is shared.

# **11\. Lead Qualification Notes**

## **Headline**

Review requests should be qualified by production path clarity.

## **Strong Fit Signals**

Company can name one production path.  
Agents are already being used or considered.  
Approval is unclear.  
Rights review arrives late.  
NILP® or talent usage is relevant.  
Assets are reused across channels.  
Protected records are needed.  
Creation Rights adoption is being considered.  
Multiple teams touch the process.  
The company has enterprise media volume.

## **Weak Fit Signals**

Only wants generic AI tips.  
Only wants a course.  
Only wants legal advice.  
Only wants a software demo.  
Only wants a creative agency.  
Cannot name a production path.  
No media creation process involved.  
No rights, approval, agent, reuse, or record issue.

## **Suggested Response for Weak Fit**

Thank you for reaching out. Creation Skills focuses on consulting around governed media creation, production agents, operating files, approval gates, rights review, protected records, and Creation Rights readiness. Based on the information provided, this may not be the right fit for a C/Skills Review at this stage.

# **15\. Final Form Rule**

## **Headline**

The first form should start the conversation, not collect the case file.

## **Body**

Creation Skills should use forms to understand context and route interest.

The detailed review happens later, through the right client process.

## **Core Intake Line**

Start with the production path.  
Do not send sensitive materials.  
Creation Skills will follow up if the review is a fit.

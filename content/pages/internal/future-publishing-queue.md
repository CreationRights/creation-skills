## **Page Identity**

**Creation Skills**  
**C/Skills.**  
**A Creation Rights Division**

## **Document Type**

Internal publishing plan

## **Public Status**

Not a public page

## **Purpose**

This page defines the staged publishing order for Creation Skills after the first launch.

The master document contains the full Creation Skills content library. The first public website should remain focused. Future pages should be released in a controlled order based on sales value, SEO value, brand clarity, and Creation Rights readiness.

The goal is to publish depth gradually without making the website feel bloated.

---

# **1\. Publishing Principle**

## **Headline**

Publish depth after the core offer is clear.

## **Body**

Creation Skills should not release every page at once.

The first launch should make the offer easy to understand:

Creation Skills is consulting.  
Creation Rights is the platform.  
CreativeIP.org is education and standards.  
The entry point is a C/Skills Review.  
The company starts with one production path.

Future pages should strengthen that foundation.

## **Rule**

Publish pages that make the offer clearer, not just bigger.

## **Eyebrow**

### **4\. Rights Review and NILP® Checks**

Why publish fifth:

This supports platform readiness and explains what Creation Rights can govern once the company process is ready.

### **6\. Asset Reuse and Usage Controls**

Why publish sixth:

This page connects rights, NILP®, paid media, localization, and records.

### **7\. Decision Rights and Escalation Paths**

Why publish seventh:

This helps enterprise buyers understand who approves, who blocks, and where unclear work goes.

### **8\. Responsible Agent Use**

### **9\. Deliverables and Operating Assets**

# **8\. Publishing Criteria**

## **Eyebrow**

CRITERIA

## **Headline**

Do not publish a page just because it exists.

## **Publish a Page When**

It supports the main offer.

It has a clear CTA.

It does not confuse Creation Skills with Creation Rights.

It does not confuse Creation Skills with CreativeIP.org.

It has a finished SEO title and description.

It has a clear URL.

It has been checked for avoided language.

It has legal review notes if needed.

It has a place in navigation, footer, resources, or internal linking.

It is visually supported by the current site design.

## **Do Not Publish When**

The page feels repetitive.

The page adds volume without clarity.

The page needs legal review and has not been reviewed.

The page creates a new service that is not ready to sell.

The page makes Creation Skills sound like generic AI consulting.

The page makes Creation Skills sound like a course provider.

The page should remain internal planning.

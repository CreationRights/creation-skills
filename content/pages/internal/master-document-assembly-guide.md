## **Purpose**

This page defines how the full Creation Skills content library should be assembled into one master document.

The master document should preserve every finished page, even if that page is not part of the first public website launch.

The goal is to keep the full Creation Skills system organized, searchable, and ready for staged publishing.

# **1\. Master Document Purpose**

## **Headline**

The master doc is the source of truth.

## **Body**

The Creation Skills master document should hold every approved page, internal planning note, utility page, resource article, and future publishing page in one organized structure.

It should not only reflect the first launch site.

It should preserve the full content library for:

Launch build  
Phase two expansion  
SEO publishing  
Sales support  
Partner planning  
Legal review  
Resource planning  
Future site updates  
Gemini design prompts  
Claude Code build prompts

## **Rule**

The public site can be selective.  
The master document should be complete.

# **5\. Launch Status Labels**

## **Headline**

Each page needs one status.

## **Labels**

### **Launch**

Build and publish in the first public website.

### **Utility Launch**

Build and publish for legal, trust, form, or site function.

### **Conditional Utility Launch**

Build only if the related form exists.

### **Optional Launch**

Publish only if design and content are polished enough.

### **Phase Two**

Publish after launch as concept depth or SEO support.

### **Phase Three**

Publish after launch as audience-specific sales and SEO pages.

### **Phase Four**

Publish later as ecosystem or resource expansion.

### **Internal Only**

Keep in the master document and do not publish.

### **Legal Review Required**

Do not publish until reviewed by counsel.

# **7\. Master Doc Formatting Rules**

## **Headline**

Keep every page easy to copy into build.

## **Rules**

Use one H1 page title per page.

Use clear section labels.

Keep metadata at the top of each page.

Keep CTA labels consistent.

Keep legal-review placeholders visible.

Keep internal-only notes clearly marked.

Do not remove pages just because they are not launching.

Do not mix multiple pages into one page section.

Do not hide important launch-status notes inside body copy.

# **10\. Build Handoff Rules**

## **Headline**

The master doc should be easy for Gemini and Claude Code to use.

## **For Gemini**

Use the brand context, visual direction, and core launch pages.

Give Gemini only the pages being designed for that round.

Do not give Gemini the entire master document if the task is only a homepage or one page.

## **For Claude Code**

Use the final launch sitemap, final menu structure, and page content.

Build from reusable components.

Do not expose internal-only pages publicly.

Do not add phase two pages to top navigation unless instructed.

## **Handoff Rule**

Master doc is the source.  
Launch sitemap controls what gets built.  
Menu structure controls what appears in navigation.  
Page cutdown plan controls what waits.

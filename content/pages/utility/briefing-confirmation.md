# **Section 2: Common Briefing Topics**

## **Eyebrow**

TOPICS

## **Headline**

Briefings are built around practical media operating questions.

## **Body**

Creation Skills briefings are not generic AI talks.

They focus on the operating layer behind modern media creation: how work moves, where agents support it, where people approve, what rights review requires, what records need to exist, and what Creation Rights should govern.

## **Topic Cards**

### **Governed Media Creation**

How companies can redesign the production path behind modern media without losing human control.

### **Company-Owned Production Agents**

Why production agents need owners, operating files, blocked actions, access limits, and human checkpoints.

### **Human Approval Gates**

How companies decide what can move, what must stop, who approves, and what record needs to exist.

### **Rights Review and NILP®**

Why rights, consent, talent usage, NILP®, paid media, and reuse checks need to sit earlier in the production path.

### **Asset Reuse and Protected Records**

How companies can manage assets that are cut down, localized, boosted, repackaged, translated, refreshed, or reused.

### **Creation Rights Readiness**

What companies need to prepare before Creation Rights carries the protected layer around rights, NILP®, consent, approvals, metadata, audit, payouts, and compliance-sensitive media records.

# **Section 3: Session Formats**

## **Eyebrow**

SESSION FORMATS

## **Headline**

Different audiences need different briefing formats.

## **Body**

Creation Skills may support several kinds of sessions depending on the audience and context.

## **Format Cards**

### **Executive Briefing**

A private session for leadership teams reviewing how media creation is changing across people, agents, approvals, rights, records, and platforms.

### **Board or Sponsor Session**

A focused session for senior sponsors considering Creation Rights adoption, enterprise media governance, or production-agent operating models.

### **Industry Talk**

A keynote, fireside chat, or panel session for media, entertainment, brand, legal, creative operations, technology, or rights-focused audiences.

### **Legal and Rights Briefing**

A session for legal, rights, compliance, and risk teams on earlier review points, NILP®, consent, talent usage, reuse, claims, and protected records.

### **Creative Operations Briefing**

A session for creative operations, marketing operations, production operations, and DAM or MAM teams on company flow, asset lifecycle, approval gates, and system repair.

### **Production-Agent Briefing**

A session for companies exploring how to bring production agents into real media work without losing ownership, review, or control.

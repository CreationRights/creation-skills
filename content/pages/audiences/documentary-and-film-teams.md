THE PROBLEM

## **Body**

Documentary and film teams manage large volumes of material.

Footage is logged.  
Transcripts are cleaned.  
Story notes are revised.  
Archive materials are reviewed.  
Subjects and talent may need approval.  
Captions and translations need review.  
Festival and distributor materials need packaging.

The problem is not that teams cannot produce the work.

The problem is that the company flow around footage, story, rights, approvals, and delivery is often hard to repeat and hard to record.

Creation Skills helps fix that layer.

# **Section 4: Company-Owned Production Agents**

## **Eyebrow**

PRODUCTION AGENTS

## **Headline**

Film production agents need jobs, owners, limits, and approval paths.

## **Body**

Production agents can help documentary and film teams prepare complex materials faster.

They can clean transcripts, summarize footage notes, prepare story notes, organize archive materials, draft captions, support pitch decks, and prepare delivery checklists.

But they need rules before they support real film materials.

A production agent should know what it can prepare, what it cannot decide, who owns the output, and when a human must approve.

## **Example Agents**

### **Transcript Cleanup Agent**

Prepares cleaned transcript drafts, speaker notes, time references, and review-ready materials.

### **Story Notes Agent**

Prepares structured story notes, character notes, timeline summaries, and scene references for producer review.

### **Archive Log Agent**

Summarizes archive references, flags missing source details, and prepares rights review notes.

### **Trailer Concept Agent**

Prepares trailer concept notes, possible structure options, and review materials for producers and editors.

### **Caption Prep Agent**

Prepares caption drafts, subtitle notes, and accessibility support materials for human review.

### **Delivery Checklist Agent**

Checks final package requirements, file naming, captions, metadata, approval records, and rights notes.

# **Section 8: Subject, Talent, and NILP® Review**

## **Eyebrow**

SUBJECT AND TALENT REVIEW

## **Headline**

People in the story need clear usage controls.

## **Body**

Documentary and film work often involves real people, performers, subjects, interviewees, artists, athletes, experts, voice, likeness, persona, and sensitive representation.

Creation Skills helps teams define when subject, talent, NILP®, consent, or legal review should happen before media moves forward.

## **Review Questions**

Is a real person identifiable?

Is name, image, likeness, persona, or voice being used?

Was consent captured?

Is the material being reused in a new context?

Is the clip moving into paid media?

Is a new territory or channel being added?

Is the usage window changing?

Is the material subject-facing?

Is the representation sensitive?

Does legal, producer, subject, or talent review apply?

## **Outcome**

Subject-facing and talent-sensitive media moves with clearer review, approval, and recordkeeping.

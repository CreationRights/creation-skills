Creation Skills helps fix that layer.

# **Section 4: Company-Owned Production Agents**

## **Eyebrow**

PRODUCTION AGENTS

## **Headline**

Broadcast agents need jobs, owners, limits, and approval paths.

## **Body**

Production agents can help broadcast and network teams prepare episode materials faster.

They can summarize transcripts, prepare recaps, suggest promo clips, draft social copy, support subtitle preparation, organize archive notes, and prepare delivery checklists.

But they need rules before they support real broadcast media.

A production agent should know what it can prepare, what it cannot decide, who owns the output, and when a human must approve.

## **Example Agents**

### **Episode Recap Agent**

Prepares draft recaps, segment summaries, internal notes, and audience-facing recap options from approved episode materials.

### **Promo Clip Agent**

Suggests possible promo moments, clip notes, hooks, captions, and platform-specific options for producer review.

### **Subtitle Prep Agent**

Prepares subtitle drafts, timing notes, language QA materials, and review packages for human approval.

### **Accessibility Copy Agent**

Prepares accessibility descriptions, audio guide notes, and support copy for review by the appropriate owner.

### **Archive Summary Agent**

Summarizes archive materials and flags missing source, rights, metadata, or reuse information before material moves forward.

### **Internal Handoff Agent**

Prepares structured handoff notes between production, edit, legal, standards, archive, accessibility, social, and delivery teams.

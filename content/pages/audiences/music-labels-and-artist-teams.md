Creation Skills helps fix that layer.

# **Section 4: Company-Owned Production Agents**

## **Eyebrow**

PRODUCTION AGENTS

## **Headline**

Music agents need jobs, owners, limits, and approval paths.

## **Body**

Production agents can help music teams prepare release materials faster.

They can draft captions, summarize artist notes, prepare playlist copy, suggest short clips, format release materials, organize catalog refreshes, and prepare market versions.

But they need rules before they support real release work.

A production agent should know what it can prepare, what it cannot decide, who owns the output, and when a human must approve.

## **Example Agents**

### **Release Asset Agent**

Prepares release copy, announcement drafts, partner notes, platform copy, and delivery checklist materials.

### **Artist Content Agent**

Prepares fan updates, artist post drafts, behind-the-scenes captions, and content options for approval.

### **Shorts Production Agent**

Prepares short clip suggestions, caption drafts, hooks, platform notes, and social package ideas.

### **Playlist Pitch Prep Agent**

Prepares playlist pitch drafts, campaign notes, artist context, and territory-specific pitch materials.

### **Merch Copy Agent**

Prepares merch descriptions, launch copy, product story notes, and commerce content for review.

### **Catalog Refresh Agent**

Prepares archive summaries, anniversary copy, market refresh ideas, short-form suggestions, and rights review notes.

### **Market Versioning Agent**

Prepares localized copy drafts, market-specific captions, territory notes, and review materials.

# **Section 8: Rights, Catalog, and Reuse**

## **Eyebrow**

RIGHTS AND REUSE

## **Headline**

Catalog and release assets need reuse rules.

## **Body**

Music assets rarely stay in one use.

A release clip becomes a paid ad.  
An artist photo becomes a market asset.  
A lyric video becomes a short-form cutdown.  
A catalog asset becomes an anniversary campaign.  
A tour post becomes a partner asset.  
A behind-the-scenes clip becomes platform content.

Creation Skills helps music teams define when reuse is allowed, when review is required, and what needs to be recorded.

## **Reuse Questions**

What was the original approved use?

Who approved it?

What rights were granted?

Was NILP® present?

Was artist likeness or voice included?

What channel was approved?

What territory was approved?

Was paid media included?

Was the usage window limited?

Has the asset changed?

Does management or label approval apply?

Does the new use require additional review?

What record should be kept?

## **Outcome**

Music teams can reuse assets with more confidence and less reliance on memory.

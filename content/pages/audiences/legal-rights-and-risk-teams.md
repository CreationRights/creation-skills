## **Headline**

A social cutdown includes talent.  
A creator post becomes paid media.  
A product claim appears in a caption.  
A voice clip gets reused.  
An archive asset enters a campaign.  
A market version changes the context.  
A production agent prepares new copy from old material.  
A team wants to reuse an approved asset in a new channel.

Legal and rights teams are then asked to make fast decisions with incomplete context.

Creation Skills helps move the review points earlier, so the company knows when media can move, when it must stop, and who needs to approve it.

# **Section 4: Company-Owned Production Agents**

## **Eyebrow**

PRODUCTION AGENTS

## **Headline**

Agents need legal and rights limits before they support real media.

## **Body**

Production agents can help teams prepare material faster, but they should not make rights decisions.

They can draft, summarize, translate, format, flag, package, and prepare.

They should not approve talent use, change rights terms, clear product claims, reuse restricted assets, approve NILP® usage, or decide what can be published.

Creation Skills helps legal and rights teams define the limits.

## **Example Agents**

### **Rights Review Prep Agent**

Flags possible rights, NILP®, talent, usage, territory, channel, format, or reuse questions before review.

### **Talent Usage Check Agent**

Identifies when a person’s name, image, likeness, persona, or voice may require approval.

### **Product Claim Flag Agent**

Flags product, performance, comparison, sustainability, health, beauty, financial, or technical claims for review.

### **Reuse Review Prep Agent**

Prepares original approval context, usage scope, territory, channel, talent status, and rights notes before reuse.

### **Contract Reminder Agent**

Summarizes approved usage terms, known restrictions, approval requirements, and escalation notes from approved company materials.

### **Delivery Record Agent**

Checks whether approval, rights, NILP®, consent, metadata, version, and delivery records appear complete before final movement.

# **Section 1: The Enterprise Problem**

## **Eyebrow**

THE PROBLEM

## **Headline**

Media production has outgrown informal company process.

## **Body**

Large media companies rarely have one simple path from idea to finished asset.

A title becomes trailers, clips, captions, promos, shorts, social edits, subtitles, accessibility copy, market versions, paid media, archive materials, internal packages, partner materials, and reused assets.

The work moves through many hands.

Files change.  
Rights questions appear.  
Talent usage needs review.  
Production agents support preparation.  
Approvals happen across teams.  
Assets are reused in new contexts.  
Records need to follow the work.

Creation Skills helps design the operating model behind that complexity.

# **Section 4: Company-Owned Production Agents**

## **Eyebrow**

PRODUCTION AGENTS

## **Headline**

Enterprise agents need owners, limits, and review paths.

## **Body**

Production agents can help large media teams prepare work faster.

They can summarize transcripts, prepare episode recaps, suggest clips, draft captions, translate copy, organize archive notes, prepare release materials, build delivery checklists, and support versioning.

But inside an enterprise, agents need rules before they support real media.

## **Example Agents**

### **Episode Recap Agent**

Prepares draft recaps, segment summaries, social copy, and accessibility notes from approved episode materials.

### **Clip Prep Agent**

Suggests possible moments for short-form edits, but cannot approve final clips, talent usage, or public posting.

### **Localization Prep Agent**

Prepares translation drafts, subtitle notes, and market version checks for human review.

### **Archive Summary Agent**

Summarizes archive materials and flags missing source, rights, or metadata information before reuse.

### **Release Packaging Agent**

Prepares release copy, market variants, asset checklists, and partner materials for approval.

## **Eyebrow**

RIGHTS AND TALENT

## **Headline**

Rights review belongs inside the production path.

## **Body**

Media and entertainment companies manage talent, rights, source material, reuse, territories, channels, formats, and usage windows across many assets.

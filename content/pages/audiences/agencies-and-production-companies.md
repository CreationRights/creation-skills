# **Section 1: The Agency Production Problem**

## **Eyebrow**

THE PROBLEM

## **Headline**

Client work now moves through too many informal handoffs.

## **Body**

Agency and production work often moves fast because people know how to improvise.

A producer finds the file.  
An account lead tracks the approval.  
An editor remembers the naming rule.  
A strategist saves the prompt.  
A creative lead knows which version is final.  
A project manager chases the client note.  
A legal or rights question appears late.

That speed can work on one project.

It becomes harder to control across many clients, campaigns, channels, markets, vendors, and asset types.

Creation Skills helps turn repeated client production paths into systems the company can own.

# **Section 4: Company-Owned Production Agents**

## **Eyebrow**

PRODUCTION AGENTS

## **Headline**

Agency agents need jobs, owners, limits, and client review paths.

## **Body**

Production agents can help agencies and production companies prepare work faster.

They can summarize briefs, prepare edit notes, draft captions, create social options, organize client feedback, prepare delivery checklists, and support versioning.

But they need rules before they support real client work.

A production agent should know what it can prepare, what it cannot decide, who owns the output, and when human approval is required.

## **Example Agents**

### **Brief Summary Agent**

Prepares structured summaries from client briefs, references, campaign goals, audience notes, and delivery requirements.

### **Edit Notes Agent**

Turns producer, editor, and client notes into structured revision lists and post-production handoff notes.

### **Social Cutdown Agent**

Prepares clip suggestions, caption drafts, hook options, thumbnail concepts, and platform format notes.

### **Client Recap Agent**

Prepares meeting recaps, feedback summaries, next steps, and client-facing review notes for human approval.

### **Asset Packaging Agent**

Checks delivery package completeness, file naming, formats, captions, metadata, and required approval notes.

### **Vendor Notes Agent**

Prepares structured instructions for production partners, editors, designers, localization teams, and delivery vendors.

# **Section 7: Client Approval and Version Control**

## **Eyebrow**

CLIENT APPROVAL

## **Headline**

Client approval needs a record, not just a message thread.

## **Body**

Agency work often depends on client review cycles.

That creates risk when the company cannot easily see which version was approved, who approved it, what changed, what was delivered, and whether the approved use still applies.

Creation Skills helps agencies design clearer client approval paths and delivery records.

## **Client Approval Questions**

What version is under review?

Who owns the client approval?

What comments were accepted?

What comments were rejected?

Was legal or brand review required?

Did the asset change after approval?

Was talent or NILP® present?

Was paid media included?

Was the final delivery package approved?

What record should be kept?

## **Outcome**

Teams can move client work forward with clearer ownership, version status, and approval history.

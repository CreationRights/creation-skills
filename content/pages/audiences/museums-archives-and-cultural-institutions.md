# **Section 1: The Cultural Media Problem**

## **Eyebrow**

THE PROBLEM

## **Headline**

Cultural media carries context, rights, and institutional responsibility.

## **Body**

Museums, archives, and cultural institutions are not just publishing content.

They are interpreting collections, representing artists and subjects, preserving context, managing rights, serving public audiences, supporting access, and protecting institutional trust.

That creates a different kind of media production challenge.

A caption needs accuracy.  
An image needs rights context.  
An audio guide needs review.  
An education pack needs accessibility.  
A public program needs institutional voice.  
A collection summary may need curatorial approval.  
An archive asset may carry usage limits.  
A retail item may need source and rights clarity.

The problem is not that cultural teams cannot produce the materials.

The problem is that the company flow around collection media, rights, approval, accessibility, and publication is often hard to repeat and hard to record.

Creation Skills helps fix that layer.

# **Section 4: Company-Owned Production Agents**

## **Eyebrow**

PRODUCTION AGENTS

## **Headline**

Cultural production agents need context, limits, and review paths.

## **Body**

Production agents can help cultural teams prepare materials faster.

They can summarize collection notes, draft captions, prepare education materials, organize archive descriptions, draft accessibility copy, prepare public program copy, and structure retail descriptions.

But they need rules before they support real cultural media.

A production agent should know what it can prepare, what it cannot decide, who owns the output, and when a human must approve.

## **Example Agents**

### **Collection Summary Agent**

Prepares draft summaries from approved collection notes, object records, archive references, and curatorial materials.

### **Image Caption Agent**

Prepares caption drafts, attribution notes, source references, and rights review flags for human approval.

### **Exhibition Copy Agent**

Prepares wall text drafts, public-facing descriptions, and interpretive copy options from approved materials.

### **Audio Guide Draft Agent**

Prepares audio guide draft segments, plain-language support, and narration notes for review.

### **Education Pack Agent**

Prepares classroom materials, learning prompts, activity notes, and education support copy.

### **Accessibility Description Agent**

Prepares accessibility descriptions, image descriptions, transcript notes, and access-support materials for review.

### **Archive Prep Agent**

Summarizes archive materials, flags missing source information, and prepares review notes before public use.

### **Retail Product Copy Agent**

Prepares bookshop, exhibition merchandise, and retail copy from approved product and collection information.

# **Section 1: The Problem**

## **Eyebrow**

THE PROBLEM

## **Headline**

Faster production can hide unclear approval.

## **Body**

When media production speeds up, approval can become harder to see.

A producer thinks brand approved it.  
Brand thinks legal reviewed it.  
Legal thinks rights already checked it.  
The social team thinks the creator usage is clear.  
The paid media team thinks the asset can be boosted.  
The agent owner thinks the output was only a draft.

The asset moves.

But the company cannot clearly explain who made the call.

That is the problem a human approval gate solves.

# **Section 3: Why It Matters**

## **Eyebrow**

WHY IT MATTERS

## **Headline**

Agents can prepare the work. They should not approve the use.

## **Body**

Production agents are useful when the task is clear.

They can help prepare material for review.

But they should not make final decisions about publication, paid media, talent usage, rights, consent, NILP®, product claims, client delivery, or reuse.

## **Core Rule**

The agent does the prep.  
The human makes the call.  
The system records both.

## **Outcome**

The company gets speed from agent-supported preparation without giving up human control over the decisions that matter.

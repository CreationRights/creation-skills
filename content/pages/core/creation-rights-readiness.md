# **Section 1: Why Readiness Matters**

## **Eyebrow**

WHY READINESS MATTERS

## **Headline**

A platform cannot fix an unclear company process by itself.

## **Body**

Many companies want a governed system for media production, rights, approvals, metadata, NILP®, and reuse.

The challenge is that the internal process is often not ready.

Files move through informal handoffs.  
Approvals happen differently by team or project.  
Rights review arrives too late.  
Production agents are tested without shared rules.  
Prompts live with individuals.  
Records are incomplete.  
Reuse decisions depend on memory.

Creation Skills helps companies clean up the company flow before the platform becomes the place where protected records are carried.

# **Section 4: What Needs to Be Ready**

## **Eyebrow**

READINESS CHECKPOINTS

## **Headline**

The company needs more than platform access.

## **Body**

Before Creation Rights can work well across the organization, the company needs a clear view of the production paths, approval roles, agent rules, rights checks, and records that should sit around the platform.

## **Readiness Checkpoints**

### **1\. Production Paths Are Defined**

The company knows how work moves from brief to production, review, approval, delivery, and reuse.

### **2\. Owners Are Named**

Each step has a responsible person or team, including agent owners, approval owners, rights owners, and delivery owners.

### **3\. Approval Gates Are Clear**

The company knows what needs human approval and what cannot move forward without review.

### **4\. Rights Checks Sit Early Enough**

Rights, NILP®, consent, talent usage, and reuse review happen before the work reaches final delivery.

### **5\. Production Agents Have Limits**

Agents have defined roles, approved tasks, blocked actions, operating files, and human checkpoints.

### **6\. Records Are Meaningful**

The company knows what must be captured before the platform carries the protected record.

### **7\. Teams Can Operate the Model**

The people using the process understand their roles, approval responsibilities, agent rules, and escalation paths.

# **Section 10: Signs the Company Is Not Ready Yet**

## **Eyebrow**

READINESS GAPS

## **Headline**

The warning signs usually show up before platform adoption.

## **Body**

Creation Skills helps companies find and repair the gaps that would make platform adoption harder.

## **Common Readiness Gaps**

No clear owner for final media approval.

Production agents are used without operating files.

Prompts are stored privately by individuals.

Rights review happens after assets are already created.

No one can explain which assets can be reused.

Files move through informal handoffs.

Approval requirements change by team or project.

Asset metadata is incomplete or inconsistent.

Talent and NILP® checks are not tied to production.

Final delivery happens without a clear record.

## **Outcome**

These gaps can be addressed before they become platform implementation problems.

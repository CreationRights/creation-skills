# **Section 2: Featured Resources**

## **Eyebrow**

FEATURED

## **Headline**

Start with the problems most teams are already feeling.

## **Featured Articles**

### **The hidden cost of scattered creative tools**

Teams often begin with useful experiments: one video tool, one translation path, one prompt library, one agent for social cuts.

The problem starts when those experiments become part of production without ownership, review paths, operating files, or approval gates.

### **Why production agents need owners, limits, and review paths**

A production agent should not be a loose prompt.

It should have a job, owner, department, approved tasks, blocked tasks, operating files, tool access, token budget, review path, risk level, and last review date.

### **Human approval gates for modern media teams**

Agents can draft, cut, translate, summarize, package, resize, format, and prepare.

People still need to approve the moments that matter: final creative, public posts, product claims, talent-facing materials, paid media, final delivery, and sensitive reuse.

### **What rights review needs before media moves**

Rights review cannot sit only at the end of production.

For modern media teams, rights, NILP®, consent, talent use, usage scope, metadata, and reuse checks need to sit inside the production path.

THE VIEW

## **Headline**

Media creation needs a company system.

## **Body**

The same pattern appears across teams.

Tools appear first.  
Prompts stay with individuals.  
Agents support useful tasks.  
Files move through informal handoffs.  
Approvals happen late.  
Rights review arrives after production.  
The company cannot always explain what happened, who approved it, or why work moved forward.

Creation Skills exists to fix that layer.

## **Core Principles**

Your agents should belong to the company.  
Your operating files should explain how work gets done.  
Your approval gates should keep people in control.  
Your rights checks should sit inside the production path.  
Your system should improve from approved work.  
Your platform should govern the protected layer.

# **Section 5: Our View**

## **Eyebrow**

OUR VIEW

## **Headline**

Media creation needs a company system.

## **Body**

The same pattern appears across companies.

A team starts with useful tools.  
A producer writes a good prompt.  
A social team tests an agent.  
An editor finds a faster production path.  
A marketer creates new asset versions.  
A legal review arrives late.  
A rights question appears after production.  
A file gets reused without enough context.

The work improves in fragments, but the company system does not.

Creation Skills turns those fragments into a governed company flow.

## **Principles**

### **Agents should belong to the company.**

Production agents should have defined jobs, owners, limits, operating files, and review paths.

### **People should approve the moments that matter.**

Agents can prepare work. Humans make the final call on sensitive, public-facing, rights-sensitive, or brand-sensitive outputs.

### **Rights review should sit inside the production path.**

Rights, NILP®, consent, talent use, metadata, usage scope, and reuse checks should not be left until the end.

### **Operating files should replace loose prompts.**

The company should own the rules, examples, blocked actions, naming conventions, handoffs, and review steps agents follow.

### **The system should improve under control.**

Agents can suggest improvements. Owners approve changes. Operating files evolve from approved work.

### **Creation Rights should govern the protected layer.**

The platform should control the sensitive records around rights, approvals, metadata, audit, consent, NILP®, and usage.

BOUNDARIES

## **Headline**

Not a tool shop. Not a course site. Not an agency.

Creation Skills has a clear role in the ecosystem.

# **Section 1: What the Review Covers**

## **Eyebrow**

WHAT WE REVIEW

## **Headline**

A current-state look at your media creation process.

## **Body**

A C/Skills Review looks at how the work actually moves today, not just how the process is meant to work on paper.

We review where work starts, where files live, who owns decisions, which tools and agents are already being used, where approval happens, where rights checks enter, and what must be recorded before delivery.

## **Review Areas**

### **Company Flow**

Brief intake, production stages, file movement, approval paths, handoffs, versioning, delivery, and reuse.

### **Team Roles**

Who owns the brief, production, rights review, brand review, legal escalation, agent supervision, delivery, and final sign-off.

### **Production Agents**

Where agents or tools are already being used, what they support, who owns them, what they can do, and what they should not do.

### **Operating Files**

Whether prompts, task rules, examples, blocked actions, naming conventions, output formats, and review paths are documented or scattered across individuals.

### **Human Approval Gates**

Who approves what, when review is required, what triggers escalation, and what cannot move forward without a human decision.

### **Rights and NILP® Review**

Where rights, consent, talent use, NILP®, usage scope, metadata, and reuse checks sit inside the production path.

### **Creation Rights Readiness**

Whether the company has the operating model, roles, records, approval paths, and internal controls needed before platform adoption or wider rollout.

# **Section 2: When to Book a Review**

## **Eyebrow**

WHEN IT FITS

## **Headline**

Book a review when the work is moving faster than the company process.

## **Body**

Most companies do not need another review because they lack tools. They need one because the work has outgrown the system around it.

## **Signs You Are Ready**

Your teams are testing agents or tools without shared rules.

Prompts and examples live with individuals instead of the company.

Approvals happen late or inconsistently.

Rights, talent, NILP®, or reuse questions appear after production starts.

Files move through too many informal handoffs.

Assets are translated, cut down, repackaged, or reused without a clear approval path.

No one can easily explain who approved what, why it moved, or what record was kept.

The company wants Creation Rights but needs the internal process ready first.

# **Section 7: What the Review Is Not**

## **Eyebrow**

BOUNDARIES

## **Headline**

Not a demo. Not a course. Not a tool audit.

## **Body**

The C/Skills Review is not a software demo or generic training session.

It is a consulting review of the company flow behind media creation.

## **Boundary Cards**

### **Not a SaaS Demo**

Creation Rights is the platform. The review looks at whether the company process is ready to run through a governed platform layer.

### **Not a CIP Course**

CreativeIP.org handles formal education and certification. Creation Skills reviews the company process and prepares the organization for operational change.

### **Not Generic Tool Advice**

We do not simply recommend tools. We review how people, agents, files, approvals, rights, and delivery should work together.

### **Not Creative Direction**

We do not replace the company’s creative team. We help design the operating model that lets the team create with more control.

# **Section 1: Why Start Here**

## **Eyebrow**

WHY START HERE

## **Headline**

Most companies do not need another tool. They need to understand the system around the work.

## **Body**

Modern media teams often have capable people, strong creative output, and useful tools. The problem is usually the layer between them.

Files move without clear ownership.  
Prompts stay with individuals.  
Agents support production without shared limits.  
Approval paths change by project.  
Rights checks happen after work is already made.  
Reuse requests arrive without enough context.  
No one can always explain who approved what, why it moved, or what record was kept.

The C/Skills Review finds those gaps before larger transformation work begins.

# **Section 2: What We Review**

## **Eyebrow**

REVIEW AREAS

## **Headline**

A current-state view of the media creation process.

## **Body**

The review looks at the real company flow behind media creation, not the version that only exists in policy documents.

## **Review Areas**

### **Company Flow**

How creative work moves from brief to production, review, approval, delivery, reuse, and recordkeeping.

### **People and Roles**

Who owns the brief, production, legal escalation, brand review, rights review, agent supervision, delivery, and final sign-off.

### **Production Agents**

Where agents or tools are already being used, what they support, who owns them, what they can do, and what they should not do.

### **Operating Files**

Whether prompts, task rules, examples, blocked actions, naming conventions, output formats, and review steps are company-owned or scattered across individuals.

### **Human Approval Gates**

Who approves what, when review is required, what triggers escalation, and what cannot move forward without a human decision.

### **Rights and NILP® Review**

Where rights, consent, talent use, NILP®, usage scope, metadata, and reuse checks sit inside the production path.

### **Creation Rights Readiness**

Whether the company has the operating model, roles, records, approval paths, and internal controls needed before platform adoption or wider rollout.

# **Section 3: What Triggers a Review**

## **Eyebrow**

WHEN IT FITS

## **Headline**

Book a review when media production is moving faster than the company process.

## **Body**

A C/Skills Review is useful when teams are already producing, versioning, approving, and reusing media at speed, but the company system around the work is not clear enough.

## **Common Triggers**

Teams are testing agents or tools without shared rules.

Prompts and examples live with individuals instead of the company.

Production agents generate useful outputs, but no one owns the review path.

Approval paths are inconsistent across teams, markets, agencies, or asset types.

Rights, talent, NILP®, or reuse questions appear after production starts.

Files move through too many informal handoffs.

Final assets are cut down, translated, repackaged, or reused without a clear approval path.

The company wants Creation Rights but needs the internal process ready first.

# **Section 8: What the Review Is Not**

## **Eyebrow**

BOUNDARIES

## **Headline**

Not a demo. Not a course. Not a tool audit.

## **Body**

The C/Skills Review is a consulting review of the company flow behind media creation.

## **Boundary Cards**

### **Not a SaaS Demo**

Creation Rights is the platform. The review looks at whether the company process is ready to run through a governed platform layer.

### **Not a CIP Course**

CreativeIP.org handles formal education and certification. Creation Skills reviews the company process and prepares the organization for operational change.

### **Not Generic Tool Advice**

We do not simply recommend tools. We review how people, agents, files, approvals, rights, and delivery should work together.

### **Not Creative Direction**

We do not replace the company’s creative team. We help design the operating model that lets the team create with more control.

# **Section 1: Why Pilot**

## **Eyebrow**

WHY PILOT

## **Headline**

Test the model before the company scales it.

## **Body**

Media creation often looks clean in a deck and messy in production.

A pilot gives the company a controlled way to test the new model with real teams, real files, real review paths, real agent support, and real approval decisions.

The pilot helps answer practical questions:

Can the team follow the new process?  
Do the agents have the right limits?  
Do the operating files explain the task clearly?  
Do people know when to approve or escalate?  
Do rights checks happen early enough?  
Are records captured before work moves forward?  
Is the company ready for Creation Rights to govern the protected layer?

# **Section 3: What the Pilot Tests**

## **Eyebrow**

WHAT GETS TESTED

## **Headline**

People, agents, approvals, rights, and records in practice.

## **Body**

The pilot tests the parts of the model that need to work together in production.

## **Test Areas**

### **Company Flow**

How the work moves from brief to production, review, approval, delivery, reuse, and records.

### **Production Agents**

Whether the agents can support the task without overstepping their limits.

### **Operating Files**

Whether the instructions, examples, blocked actions, formats, naming rules, and review steps are clear enough.

### **Human Approval Gates**

Whether people know what they approve, when they approve, and what requires escalation.

### **Rights and NILP® Checks**

Whether rights, consent, talent use, NILP®, usage scope, metadata, and reuse review happen at the right points.

### **Records**

Whether decisions, approvals, asset status, rights notes, and delivery outcomes are captured clearly.

### **Creation Rights Readiness**

Whether the production path is ready to move into the Creation Rights platform layer.

# **Section 10: Pilot Examples**

## **Eyebrow**

EXAMPLES

## **Headline**

Different production paths. Same need for control.

## **Example Cards**

### **Music Release Assets**

Agents prepare captions, short clips, fan updates, playlist pitch drafts, merch copy, and market versions. People approve the outputs. Creation Rights governs rights, NILP®, metadata, approvals, and audit before media moves.

### **Broadcast Episode Packaging**

Agents prepare recaps, subtitles, promo versions, social copy, accessibility copy, and internal handoffs. Producers and reviewers approve the package before delivery.

### **Product Media**

Agents prepare product video outlines, image briefs, PDP copy, marketplace versions, ad variants, feature breakdowns, and social packages. Brand, legal, and product owners approve the final media.

### **Fashion Campaign Delivery**

Agents prepare lookbook drafts, collection notes, styling notes, product copy, editorial captions, campaign variants, and market assets. Creative, brand, legal, and market teams approve where required.

### **Documentary Production Support**

Agents prepare transcript cleanup, story notes, character notes, archive logs, pitch materials, captions, translations, and delivery checklists. Producers and rights reviewers approve before the work moves.

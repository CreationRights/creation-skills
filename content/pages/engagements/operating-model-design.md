# **Section 1: Why It Matters**

## **Eyebrow**

WHY IT MATTERS

## **Headline**

Creative production needs a company system.

## **Body**

Modern media moves through many hands before it reaches an audience.

A brief becomes source files.  
Source files become edits, versions, captions, translations, thumbnails, product media, release assets, campaign variants, and delivery packages.  
People review.  
Agents prepare.  
Legal checks.  
Brand approves.  
Talent usage is confirmed.  
Assets are reused, repackaged, and sent into new channels.

Without a clear operating model, the company relies on informal judgment, individual memory, loose prompts, scattered files, and late-stage review.

Operating Model Design fixes the structure around the work.

# **Section 5: Agent-Supported Production**

## **Eyebrow**

PRODUCTION AGENTS

## **Headline**

Agents support the model. They do not replace it.

## **Body**

Operating Model Design defines where company-owned production agents can support the work and where human decision-making remains required.

Agents may help draft, cut, summarize, translate, format, package, resize, route, and prepare. But the company still needs named owners, operating files, blocked actions, approval gates, and records.

## **Agent Design Questions**

Which tasks can agents support?

Who owns each agent?

What files can the agent access?

What examples can the agent learn from?

What outputs can the agent prepare?

What actions are blocked?

When must a human review the output?

What triggers legal, brand, talent, or rights review?

What record must be kept?

## **Core Line**

The agent does the prep.  
The human makes the call.  
The system records both.

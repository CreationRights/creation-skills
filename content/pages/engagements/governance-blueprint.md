# **Section 1: Why Governance Matters**

## **Eyebrow**

WHY IT MATTERS

## **Headline**

Creative work should not move on informal approval.

## **Body**

In many companies, media moves because someone knows the usual process, remembers the right person, finds the old brief, or assumes an asset is safe to reuse.

That works until the volume grows.

More assets.  
More versions.  
More agents.  
More talent.  
More channels.  
More reuse.  
More approvals.  
More records required.

Governance Blueprint gives the company a clearer rule set for how media should move, when it should stop, and who has authority to approve it.

# **Section 2: What We Define**

## **Eyebrow**

THE GOVERNANCE SCOPE

## **Headline**

Decision rights, approval gates, review triggers, and protected records.

## **Body**

Creation Skills designs the governance structure around the company’s media creation process.

The goal is not to create paperwork. The goal is to make the rules clear enough that people, agents, and platforms can support the work without losing control.

## **Governance Areas**

### **Decision Rights**

Who has authority to approve creative, brand, legal, rights, talent, reuse, delivery, and exception decisions.

### **Approval Gates**

Where human review is required before work can move into public, client-facing, paid, licensed, reused, or delivered contexts.

### **Rights Review Triggers**

When rights, NILP®, consent, usage scope, talent use, metadata, territory, format, or channel review is required.

### **Production-Agent Limits**

What agents can prepare, what they cannot decide, and when an output must be escalated to a person.

### **Reuse Controls**

How existing assets can be refreshed, localized, cut down, repackaged, translated, or reissued.

### **Escalation Paths**

Where unclear, risky, incomplete, or disputed work goes before it can move forward.

### **Record Requirements**

What must be documented before delivery, reuse, approval, or platform entry.

# **Section 8: Reuse Controls**

## **Eyebrow**

ASSET REUSE

## **Headline**

Reuse should have a rule set.

## **Body**

Modern media assets are rarely used once.

They are cut down, refreshed, localized, repackaged, translated, edited, placed into paid media, reused in product pages, inserted into pitch materials, or revived for new markets.

Governance Blueprint defines how reuse should be reviewed before it happens.

## **Reuse Questions**

What is the original approved use?

Who approved it?

What rights were granted?

What territory was covered?

What channel was covered?

What format was covered?

Was talent or NILP® present?

Was the usage window limited?

Has the asset been modified?

Does the new use require additional approval?

What record should be kept?

## **Outcome**

The company has a clearer path for repurposing assets without relying on assumptions.
